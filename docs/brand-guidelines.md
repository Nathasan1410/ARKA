# ARKA Brand Guidelines

## 1. Brand Identity

**ARKA** is an intelligent reconciliation and audit system. It acts as the bridge between business intent (orders) and physical reality (inventory movements), providing deterministic triage and immutable proof.

The brand should feel:
- **Authoritative but not accusatory:** We are here to surface facts and assist operators, not to police or punish.
- **Modern & Technical:** A cutting-edge tool utilizing Web3 proofs (0G) and AI (OpenClaw), presented through a clean, Web2-friendly interface.
- **Transparent:** Processes, reasoning, and proof status are always visible and explainable.

## 2. Voice and Tone

The language used in ARKA interfaces, documentation, and simulated agent responses must adhere to strict "audit-safe" principles.

### We are:
- **Factual:** "Movement recorded 99g whey."
- **Objective:** "Usage above expected range."
- **Action-Oriented:** "Review immediately or request explanation."

### We are not:
- **Emotional or Accusatory:** Never use words like *theft, fraud, culprit, guilty, or stolen.*
- **Overpromising:** Do not claim "AI proves theft" or "Automatic punishment."
- **Vague:** Avoid "Something went wrong." Specify *what* mismatched.

### Examples of Tone:
- **Instead of:** "Admin Panel" -> **Use:** "Operator Console"
- **Instead of:** "Simulate Theft" -> **Use:** "Run Critical Review"
- **Instead of:** "Evidence of Fraud" -> **Use:** "Order & Evidence"

## 3. Brand Visuals

ARKA's visual presence relies heavily on its UI as the primary brand touchpoint.

- **Logo Usage (Conceptual):** The logo should be simple, geometric, and convey concepts of matching, balance, or chains (representing reconciliation and anchoring). It should be rendered in the primary brand color (Indigo) or monochrome.
- **Color Palette:** 
  - **Primary:** Indigo (`#4f46e5`) representing intelligence, technology, and trust.
  - **Support:** Slate grays for structure, pure whites for clarity, and standard traffic-light semantic colors (Emerald, Amber, Red) for status.
- **Whitespace:** Generous use of whitespace is a core brand asset. It implies that the system is unburdened, fast, and easy to use.

## 4. The "Operator-First" Principle

Every touchpoint should respect the operator's time and cognitive load. 
- Avoid complex navigational trees. 
- Surface the most critical discrepancy immediately.
- Clearly differentiate between *what happened* (Evidence), *what ARKA thinks* (Triage), and *where it is stored* (Proof).
- Buttons and calls-to-action should explicitly describe the outcome (e.g., "Approve explanation request" instead of just "Submit").
